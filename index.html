About

The South Sound FC was one of the founding members of the EPLWA and we looked forward to the high level competition it will bring to our state. We have seen much interest in this league and a lot of desire to participate both from the clubs and the players in the area. EPLWA’s inaugural year ran from May-July, 2014.

We ran a team during the two seasons prior to the start of EPLWA without an organized league. We worked to set up games in preparation for the U23 USASA regional competition and to build our club for this level of player. Our teams played in the national championship tournament for U23s both in 2012 and 2013. The ideas that formulated this league came from the lack of regional competition at this level and the need for it. We were excited to continue our quest to develop players. We will continue to focus on the U23 age group as we see relevance to what the PDL is doing and we believe we can support it well. We intend however to also keep older players to build competition and for leadership purposes.

We have a lot of work to do and are always interested in speaking with any groups when it comes to organizing supporter groups, sponsorship and community outreach specialists and film/photography interns. Any interested parties should email John Crouch at ssoundfc@gmail.com.

Highlights

South Sound looked to take advantage of a Shadow squad that played the night before in a 3-0 victory at WestSound and had several early chances. An offside call negated what they thought might have been their opening tally in the first 10 minutes and a nice play by Adrian Rubio finished with a shot just wide of the target moments later.

Shortly after the South Sound opener, Daniel Gray nearly doubled the lead for the hosts with a chip shot, but Shadow netminder Cody Lang parried it over the bar.

The Shadow came out in the second half with a little more action in the offensive end looking to earn at least a point, but it was South Sound that had the best chance of the half – prior to the Shadow goal – in the 72nd minute when a ball was crossed in and knocked away by Lang. The clearance, however, went directly to a KC Philips who headed it back toward the open goal only to see Elliot Fauske clear it off the line with a header of his own.

Both sides finished fairly even statistically with the Shadow posting seven shots to South Sound’s six and each keeper making five saves.
